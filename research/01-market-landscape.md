# Market Landscape

## Market Name

Recommended category language:

**Context recovery for desktop knowledge work.**

Secondary names:

- Attention HUD.
- Work continuity tool.
- Breadcrumb journaling.
- Hotkey-driven focus recovery.
- Interstitial journaling for work.

The market exists at the intersection of five visible software categories:

1. ADHD and neurodivergent planning tools.
2. Prosumer productivity and daily planning.
3. Time tracking and focus analytics.
4. Journaling and PKM.
5. AI memory and screen history.

The deficit is between passive memory and active planning. People do not only need to know what happened or what they planned. They need to recover **what they meant to do next**.

## Why Now

### Adult ADHD Is Large And Increasingly Visible

CDC reported that in 2023 an estimated **15.5 million U.S. adults**, or **6.0%**, had a current ADHD diagnosis. About **55.9%** of adults with current ADHD received their diagnosis in adulthood. About **36.5%** were not receiving treatment at the time of survey. Source: [CDC MMWR adult ADHD report](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/wr/mm7340a1.htm).

CDC also reported that among adults with ADHD taking stimulant medication, **71.5%** had difficulty filling prescriptions because medication was unavailable. Source: [CDC adult ADHD facts](https://www.cdc.gov/adhd/php/adults/index.html).

Ohio State reported a 2024 survey where **25% of U.S. adults suspected they had undiagnosed ADHD**, but only **13%** had discussed it with a doctor. Source: [Ohio State Wexner Medical Center](https://wexnermedical.osu.edu/mediaroom/pressreleaselisting/survey-finds-25-percent-of-adults-suspect-they-have-undiagnosed-).

Implication: the broad audience is larger than formal diagnosis. Messaging should be ADHD-aware but not diagnosis-dependent.

### Knowledge Work Is Structurally Fragmented

Gallup reports that among U.S. remote-capable jobs, **26%** are exclusively remote, **52%** hybrid, and **22%** on-site. Gallup also notes that about five in ten full-time U.S. employees have remote-capable jobs. Source: [Gallup Hybrid Work indicator](https://www.gallup.com/401384/indicator-hybrid-work.aspx).

Microsoft's Work Trend Index reports that **68%** of people say they do not have enough uninterrupted focus time and **62%** struggle with too much time spent searching for information. Source: [Microsoft Work Trend Index 2023](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/worklab/work-trend-index/will-ai-fix-work).

Harvard Business Review summarized research across 20 teams and 137 users in three Fortune 500 companies. Workers toggled between apps and websites nearly **1,200 times per day**, spending just under **four hours per week** reorienting after toggles, about **9%** of annual work time. Source: [HBR app toggling research](https://hbr.org/2022/08/how-much-time-and-energy-do-we-waste-toggling-between-applications).

Implication: context loss is not a niche ADHD-only problem. ADHD is the acute wedge, but fragmented desktop work is the larger market.

### Attention Recovery Is A Real Pain

UC Irvine's Gloria Mark has reported that people average about **47 seconds** on any one screen before shifting attention, and it can take up to about **25 minutes** to return attention to a project after interruption. Source: [UC Irvine profile on digital distraction research](https://ics.uci.edu/2023/01/26/regaining-focus-in-a-world-of-digital-distractions/).

In interruption research, Mark and colleagues found that people may compensate for interruptions by working faster, but experience more stress, frustration, time pressure, and effort. Source: [The Cost of Interrupted Work](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221518077_The_cost_of_interrupted_work_More_speed_and_stress).

Implication: selling "more productivity" is weaker than selling "lower re-entry cost and less mental archaeology."

### Interstitial Journaling Has Existing Pull

Interstitial journaling is already recognized in productivity and note-taking communities. Ness Labs describes it as a way to combine notes, to-dos, and time tracking by writing short notes at task transitions. Source: [Ness Labs interstitial journaling explainer](https://nesslabs.com/interstitial-journaling).

Implication: the product can ride an existing technique, but it should productize the missing layer: OS-level capture, HUD visibility, and recovery.

## Existing Budget Pools

The user already pays for some combination of:

- ADHD planner apps: Tiimo, Structured, Llama Life, Amazing Marvin.
- Premium planners: Sunsama, Akiflow, Motion.
- Time/focus tracking: RescueTime, Rize, Toggl Track.
- Desktop workflow tools: Raycast, Alfred, Obsidian Sync.
- Journals/notes: Day One, Reflect, Obsidian.
- AI memory or screen recall: Screenpipe, Recall-like tools.

This suggests willingness to pay exists, but each category solves a different problem:

- ADHD planners organize routines and schedules.
- Premium planners organize priorities and calendars.
- Trackers analyze past behavior.
- Journals store reflections.
- Launchers accelerate commands.
- AI memory stores passive history.

The opportunity is a product that preserves **active intent**.

## Market Size Logic

Do not overfit to a single TAM number. Use layered market logic:

### Acute Wedge

U.S. adults with diagnosed ADHD: 15.5 million. Not all are knowledge workers or paid software buyers. A small portion of this population can still support a strong prosumer product.

### Practical SAM

Desktop-heavy ADHD or ADHD-adjacent knowledge workers in the U.S., Canada, Europe, and Australia. This includes diagnosed ADHD, self-suspected ADHD, autism/ADHD overlap, anxiety-driven context loss, and general attention fragmentation.

### Expansion Market

All remote/hybrid knowledge workers who lose context across meetings, chat, browser tabs, tickets, docs, and code.

### Budget Proxy

Third-party estimates place productivity management software in the tens of billions globally and time tracking software in the single-digit billions with growth projected. Sources: [Grand View productivity management software](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/productivity-management-software-market), [Grand View time tracking software](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/time-tracking-software-market-report).

Use these as category-budget signals, not as exact market sizing for this product.

## Market Deficit

The deficit can be stated simply:

**There is no trusted, low-friction, desktop-native way to preserve and recover intent while work is happening.**

Existing tools either require too much planning, too much review, too much setup, too much surveillance, or too much cognitive load. This product should be the small layer that makes the current thread visible.

## Recommended Category Claim

Primary:

**A desktop attention HUD that helps you recover your train of thought.**

Secondary:

**Interstitial journaling, built into your workflow.**

Avoid:

- "ADHD treatment."
- "Medical-grade focus improvement."
- "AI employee productivity monitoring."
- "All-in-one productivity OS."

The best category is narrow, emotionally clear, and practical.
