# Target Users And Positioning

## Recommended Beachhead

Start with:

**Prosumer, keyboard-first knowledge workers with ADHD or ADHD-like executive dysfunction who do deep work on a desktop all day.**

Best first segments:

- Software engineers.
- Technical founders and indie hackers.
- Consultants and fractional operators.
- Writers, researchers, and analysts.
- Designers and product managers.

These users have:

- Acute context-loss pain.
- Existing hotkey habits.
- Permission to install desktop tools.
- Willingness to pay for productivity software.
- Public communities where workflows spread.

## Why Not Start Broad B2C ADHD

Broad consumer ADHD is real and large, but it pulls the product toward:

- Coaching.
- Routines.
- Medication and symptom language.
- Mood tracking.
- Habit programs.
- Mobile-first UX.
- Support-heavy onboarding.

That creates regulatory and product complexity before the core habit is proven.

## Why Not Start B2B

B2B/team can come later, but starting there creates immediate demands:

- Admin controls.
- SSO.
- Data retention policies.
- Employee surveillance concerns.
- Procurement.
- Security reviews.
- Disability/accommodation language.

The better path is bottom-up prosumer adoption first, then team packs for engineering/product teams once privacy and admin foundations are solid.

## Personas

### ADHD Software Engineer

Pain:

- Loses context after Slack, CI failures, meetings, code review, branch switching, and browser research.
- Has many partial threads open at once.
- Often remembers the problem but not the next small move.

Current stack:

- IDE, terminal, GitHub, Linear/Jira, Slack, browser tabs, notes, Raycast/Alfred.

Killer promise:

**Every time you switch context, leave yourself a runnable breadcrumb.**

### Solo Founder

Pain:

- Constant role switching: product, sales, support, code, writing, ops.
- No manager to impose structure.
- Task apps become another neglected system.

Killer promise:

**Keep a living flight path through chaos without becoming a project manager of yourself.**

### Consultant Or Fractional Operator

Pain:

- Jumps between clients and meetings.
- Needs to remember where each client thread stood.
- May need light end-of-day accountability notes.

Killer promise:

**One hotkey to capture where you are, what changed, and what to do when you return.**

### Writer, Researcher, Analyst

Pain:

- Deep thinking context is fragile.
- Notes sprawl across tabs, docs, papers, and scratchpads.
- Interruptions make the work feel expensive to resume.

Killer promise:

**Re-enter a thinking thread in seconds.**

### Neurodivergent Employee In A Corporate Tool Mess

Pain:

- Meetings, chat, unclear priorities, and app switching create overload.
- Needs a private support layer, not a manager dashboard.

This is a strong later market, especially for neuroinclusion, but not the first wedge.

## Jobs To Be Done

- When I am interrupted, help me preserve the state of my work so I can resume without shame or mental archaeology.
- When I drift into another tab or task, help me remember the original path.
- When I am between tasks, help me choose one concrete next action.
- When my day becomes nonlinear, show me what I was doing, why, and where to return.
- When I finish the day, give me a record of real progress without making me maintain a project system.

## Positioning Options

### Strongest

**A desktop HUD for never losing the thread.**

Why it works:

- Clear emotional pain.
- Not over-medicalized.
- Works for ADHD and non-ADHD users.
- Fits the current Tauri overlay/hotkey architecture.

### ADHD-Aware

**A breadcrumb trail for interrupted minds.**

Why it works:

- Warm and human.
- Good for landing pages and creator channels.
- Avoids "treats ADHD" language.

### Developer Wedge

**A stack trace for your attention.**

Why it works:

- Memorable for developers.
- Makes the product's mental model instantly legible.
- Could be too narrow for the main brand, but excellent for campaign copy.

### Technique-Based

**Interstitial journaling, built into your workflow.**

Why it works:

- Captures the method.
- Good for SEO and productivity communities.
- Less emotionally sharp than "never lose the thread."

## Messaging To Test

- "Never lose the thread."
- "One hotkey to mark context, one glance to recover it."
- "Your workday is not linear. This gives you a path back."
- "A private breadcrumb trail for desktop work."
- "Built for the interruption, not the ideal schedule."
- "Intentional memory, not surveillance."
- "Capture what you meant to do before the thought disappears."

## Messaging To Avoid

- "Treats ADHD."
- "Reduces ADHD symptoms."
- "Clinically proven executive function improvement."
- "Alternative to medication."
- "Employee productivity monitoring."
- "Records everything you do."
- "Fix procrastination forever."

## Acquisition Channels

### Developer Productivity

- Hacker News.
- Product Hunt.
- Indie Hackers.
- Raycast, Obsidian, Logseq, Linear, GitHub, and VS Code communities.
- Developer newsletters.
- SEO around "ADHD programmer productivity", "context switching developer", "resume deep work", and "interstitial journaling app."

### ADHD And Neurodivergent Work

- ADHD productivity creators.
- Neurodivergent tech communities.
- Body-doubling communities.
- ADHD work coaches and newsletters.
- Careful creator partnerships with evidence-aware messaging.

Note: ADHD social content can contain misinformation, so claims and creator scripts should be conservative. Source context: [OPB/NPR on ADHD TikTok and misinformation](https://www.opb.org/article/2025/03/30/adhd-is-big-on-tiktok-you-can-find-community-but-also-misleading-advice/).

### Template-Led Growth

Publish workflows:

- "Hotkey journaling for engineers."
- "The ADHD founder workday HUD."
- "How to come back after meetings without losing your thread."
- "My interstitial journaling setup for deep work."
- "A debugging stack trace for your attention."

### Bottom-Up Workplace

Later:

- "Expense this as a productivity tool."
- Team packs for engineering/product teams.
- Neuroinclusion-friendly support without becoming HR wellness software.

## Positioning Decision

Use this hierarchy:

1. Main category: desktop attention HUD.
2. Main promise: never lose the thread.
3. Method: interstitial journaling plus hotkeys.
4. Beachhead: ADHD/ADHD-adjacent knowledge workers.
5. Expansion: all interrupted desktop knowledge workers.
