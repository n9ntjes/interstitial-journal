# Central Voting System

Date: 2026-04-27

## Purpose

This file is the central scorecard for the second debate round.

It combines:

- the three persona belief files,
- the three rebuttal/debate files,
- their explicit ranked votes,
- their strongest points,
- the unresolved tensions that a judge must decide.

The three personas are:

- **Originalist**: defends the first product definition and broader market landscape.
- **Revisionist**: defends the sharper post-debate Return Notes / paid-beta strategy.
- **Freeware Heretic**: defends local-first trust, free or nearly free launch, and delayed monetization.

## The Three Strategies

### Strategy A: Originalist

Core idea:

Build a private desktop attention HUD for ADHD and ADHD-adjacent knowledge workers who lose the thread during computer work. Keep the visible path, the emotional grammar, the broader continuity promise, and the ambition to be more than a small interruption utility.

Representative line:

> "The real product is still this: a desktop companion for people who disappear from their own work and need a humane, visible way back."  
> Source: [originalist/debate.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/originalist/debate.md)

### Strategy B: Revisionist

Core idea:

Build a local-first return-to-work tool for interrupted high-context technical work. Use Return Notes or Work Save Points as the core object, start with technical founders and senior developers, run a paid beta, and prove repeated rescue behavior before broadening.

Representative line:

> "A tool that catches the moment work breaks, leaves a real trail back, and earns the right to expand because users come back faster and pay to keep that feeling."  
> Source: [revisionist/debate.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/revisionist/debate.md)

### Strategy C: Freeware Heretic

Core idea:

Launch the local-first core for free or nearly free, because trust, habit formation, and distribution matter more than early subscription revenue. Monetize later through sync, lifetime Pro, supporter licenses, onboarding, templates, or ecosystem layers.

Representative line:

> "Free because this product needs repeated lived proof before it earns pricing power."  
> Source: [freeware-heretic/debate.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/freeware-heretic/debate.md)

## Where They Agree

### 1. The product should not be sold as generic "interstitial journaling software"

All three agree that the method is real, but the buyer-facing promise must be more concrete.

Common alternatives:

- "Never lose the thread."
- "Resume work without rebuilding context."
- "Save your place before work breaks."

Sources:

- [originalist/beliefs.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/originalist/beliefs.md)
- [revisionist/beliefs.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/revisionist/beliefs.md)
- [freeware-heretic/beliefs.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/freeware-heretic/beliefs.md)

### 2. The product's core object is stronger as a Return Note or save-point-like object

All three, even the Originalist, concede that a vague "entry" or "punch" is too weak. The app should preserve a path back into work.

Shared shape:

- what thread was I in?
- what is the next visible action?
- why does it matter?
- what context should return with me?

### 3. Technical founders and senior developers are the cleanest first proving ground

Agreement:

- Their context is concrete.
- Their pain is expensive.
- They already live in hotkeys, tabs, tickets, branches, docs, Slack, meetings, and browser research.

Disagreement:

- Whether this is only a beachhead or should define the product more deeply.

### 4. Local-first trust is non-negotiable

All three agree on:

- no account required to begin, or at least no heavy account wall
- obvious export
- no surveillance theater
- no passive recording by default
- user-owned data posture

### 5. The rescue loop is the real proof

All three agree the product lives or dies on one moment:

The user gets interrupted, comes back, and knows exactly what to do next.

Shared concern:

- if people just "like the idea" but do not repeatedly use it, the strategy fails

## Main Disagreements

### Disagreement 1: Breadth vs sharpness

Originalist says:

- The broader continuity vision is the soul of the product.
- A visible path, environmental scaffolding, and emotional grammar matter.
- Narrowing too early risks winning a beta and losing the category.

Revisionist says:

- Breadth creates too many false doors.
- The first launch must test one narrow promise with one narrow buyer.
- Strategy that cannot be falsified quickly is dangerous.

Freeware Heretic says:

- Breadth matters emotionally, but the real launch should be narrow and habit-driven.
- Scope is especially dangerous if it arrives before trust and adoption.

### Disagreement 2: Is the HUD central or secondary?

Originalist:

- The HUD and visible breadcrumb path are part of the product's functional dignity.

Revisionist:

- Timing matters more than persistent surfaces.
- The product should not hide lack of proof behind elegant interface worldview.

Freeware Heretic:

- Visible path matters eventually, but early surface sprawl can become homework.

### Disagreement 3: When to charge

Originalist:

- Do not hard-gate basic use too early, but paid founding support should exist early enough to test seriousness.

Revisionist:

- Paid beta is the cleanest filter against wishful thinking.

Freeware Heretic:

- Free local core should come first; payment should sit behind sync, depth, or time, not in front of the first rescue loop.

### Disagreement 4: What pricing proves

Revisionist:

- Payment is diagnostic.

Freeware Heretic:

- Early payment mostly proves who tolerates buying tools, not who will build dependence.

Originalist:

- Payment helps, but trust and product soul must not be sacrificed to pricing neatness.

## Strongest Arguments By Persona

### Strongest Originalist Arguments

- The deeper pain is not just interruption; it is dislocation, drift, shame, and self-lostness.
- A visible path can be environmental scaffolding, not just decorative UI.
- The broader ADHD/ADHD-adjacent continuity problem is real and should remain the eventual category.
- The current codebase architecture already wants to be an OS-level continuity companion.

Best citation:

> "The problem is not merely interruption. The problem is dislocation."  
> Source: [originalist/debate.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/originalist/debate.md)

### Strongest Revisionist Arguments

- A buyer does not buy a HUD; a buyer buys relief from re-entry friction.
- The smallest useful proof is repeated rescue behavior at rupture points.
- Broad category framing can invite the wrong expectations too early.
- Payment plus retention is a better antidote to wishful thinking than free applause.

Best citation:

> "Behavior. Did the user leave a usable return path when the thread broke?"  
> Source: [revisionist/debate.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/revisionist/debate.md)

### Strongest Freeware Heretic Arguments

- Architecture is part of the message: local-first, exportable, account-light software is itself the trust wedge.
- A habit product may need adoption and lived proof before it earns pricing power.
- Freeware culture can spread a behavioral meme faster than a gated beta.
- The product can still become sustainable later through sync, lifetime Pro, supporters, templates, onboarding, or ecosystem layers.

Best citation:

> "The first interaction is a local app that works offline, stores plaintext or encrypted local files, exports to Markdown/JSON, and never requires an account."  
> Source: [freeware-heretic/beliefs.md](/Users/beherit/Sites/htdocs/ij/research/agent-debate-02/freeware-heretic/beliefs.md)

## Highest-Value Borrowings

### What The Originalist Borrowed

- `Return Note` as buyer-facing object.
- Technical founders and senior developers as first wedge.
- Rupture-point prompting.
- Behavioral proof standards.
- Local-first trust posture.

### What The Revisionist Borrowed

- The product needs more than a dead note archive.
- Emotional accuracy matters, not just saved seconds.
- The developer wedge is not the final destiny.
- Local-first trust should be obvious before money gets loud.

### What The Freeware Heretic Borrowed

- Return Note as the best core object.
- Next visible action as the center of the loop.
- Technical founders and senior developers as first stress test.
- Rescue behavior as the real metric.
- Broad ADHD branding should not lead the launch.

## Provisional Read Before The Judge

The central tension is no longer "What is the product?"

The central tension is:

**Do we optimize first for sharper proof, or for broader trust and adoption?**

The Originalist protects the soul.

The Revisionist protects falsifiability.

The Freeware Heretic protects trust and spread.

The best next strategy is likely to be a hybrid, but the judge must decide:

- which core object leads,
- how narrow the first wedge should be,
- whether basic use is paid, free, or trial-based,
- how much of the broader HUD/continuity vision survives the first build.
