# Pivot Options

## Recommendation

The best path is not a hard pivot away from the current app. It is a sharpening:

**From interstitial journal to desktop attention HUD for context recovery.**

The current implementation can support this. The strategic move is to make re-entry the center of the product, not journaling history or stats.

## Option 1: Prosumer Attention HUD

Target:

- ADHD and ADHD-adjacent desktop knowledge workers.

Promise:

- "Never lose the thread."

Core:

- Hotkey entries.
- Breadcrumb rail.
- `Lost` re-entry card.
- Next-step capture.
- Parked distractions.

Pros:

- Strong emotional pain.
- Fits current architecture.
- Clear differentiation.
- Avoids B2B procurement.
- Strong willingness to pay.

Cons:

- Requires habit formation.
- Users must feel value quickly.
- Needs excellent onboarding.

Verdict:

**Recommended primary strategy.**

## Option 2: Developer Attention Stack Trace

Target:

- Developers, support engineers, DevOps, technical founders.

Promise:

- "A stack trace for your attention."

Core:

- Thread tied to repo/branch/ticket.
- VS Code/JetBrains extension.
- GitHub/Linear/Jira integration.
- "When I come back, run/test/check X."
- Context snapshot with current file, branch, issue, PR.

Pros:

- Very acute pain.
- Hotkey-native audience.
- Easy to explain in developer language.
- Strong community distribution.

Cons:

- Narrower than full market.
- Integrations become important quickly.
- Risk of becoming a dev-tool niche.

Verdict:

**Best campaign wedge or first vertical.**

## Option 3: Meeting Re-entry Tool

Target:

- Remote/hybrid knowledge workers with meeting-heavy days.

Promise:

- "Leave every meeting with your previous thread intact."

Core:

- Before meeting: capture return note.
- After meeting: re-entry card.
- Meeting notes integration with calendar/Granola/Otter.
- "Resume previous thread" button.

Pros:

- Clear pain for almost all knowledge workers.
- B2B-friendly later.
- Easy demo.

Cons:

- Crowded meeting-assistant ecosystem.
- Less ADHD-specific.
- Could become a calendar add-on instead of a HUD.

Verdict:

**Strong feature and later positioning, not the whole product at first.**

## Option 4: Intentional Memory, Not Surveillance

Target:

- Privacy-conscious users who want AI memory without screen recording.

Promise:

- "Remember what mattered without recording everything."

Core:

- User-authored entries.
- Optional app/window metadata.
- Semantic search.
- AI summaries.
- No screenshots by default.
- Local-first storage.

Pros:

- Differentiates from Recall and lifelogging.
- Privacy-positive.
- Fits rising AI memory interest.

Cons:

- AI memory market is noisy.
- "Memory" can feel vague.
- Users may compare against more magical passive tools.

Verdict:

**Excellent differentiation pillar. Use inside main strategy.**

## Option 5: ADHD Work Accommodation Layer

Target:

- Employers supporting neurodivergent knowledge workers.

Promise:

- "Private focus infrastructure for employees who need work continuity support."

Core:

- Individual-owned HUD.
- Privacy-preserving team license.
- Accessibility features.
- Optional manager-safe progress summaries controlled by employee.

Pros:

- Larger contracts.
- Neuroinclusion is increasingly visible.
- Strong employer narrative when done carefully.

Cons:

- Procurement and compliance.
- High trust burden.
- Medical/disability claim risk.
- Employee surveillance concerns.

Verdict:

**Second act. Do not start here.**

## Option 6: Broad ADHD Planner

Target:

- General ADHD consumer audience.

Promise:

- "Plan and manage your day with ADHD."

Core:

- Calendar.
- Routines.
- Reminders.
- AI task breakdown.
- Habits.
- Mood.

Pros:

- Large visible audience.
- Proven demand from Tiimo, Structured, Llama Life.

Cons:

- Crowded.
- Pulls away from desktop HUD advantage.
- Lower differentiation.
- Higher support burden.

Verdict:

**Avoid as primary strategy.**

## Pivot Attractiveness Matrix

| Pivot | Pain Intensity | Differentiation | Fit With Current App | Revenue Potential | Start Now? |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Prosumer Attention HUD | High | High | High | High | Yes |
| Developer Attention Stack Trace | Very high | High | Medium | Medium/high | As wedge |
| Meeting Re-entry Tool | High | Medium | Medium | Medium/high | As feature |
| Intentional AI Memory | Medium/high | High | Medium | High | As pillar |
| ADHD Work Accommodation | High | Medium | Medium | High | Later |
| Broad ADHD Planner | Medium | Low | Low | Medium | No |

## Final Pivot Thesis

The product should pivot away from "journal as archive" and toward **journal as live pathfinding**.

The entries are not the destination. The destination is the user's ability to re-enter work without feeling lost.
